Disagree and Commit Describe a time when you had to make a decision that was not popular but was necessary for compliance or security reasons. How did you handle the situation and what was the outcome? In compliance with the personal data protection law and due to the outdated and insecure nature of the fingerprint entrance system at Gebruderweiss logistics company, there was a need to transition to a safer alternative. Additionally, the Office Plaza, where the company is located, had its own card reader system managed by the plaza management. 1. Researched the implications of the personal data protection law.
2. Reached out to multiple vendors for card reader systems offers.
3. Checked compatibility with the plaza's system.
4. Evaluated and selected the most suitable vendor.
5. Coordinated the installation of the card reader system.
6. Integrated the new system with the Office Plaza's card system.
7. Set up the provided software for card access management.
8. Conducted a training session for the HR department.
9. Collected feedback post-implementation.
10. Established a regular maintenance schedule with the vendor. 		Provided a secure and compliant entrance system for the company, ensuring a seamless experience for the employees.
